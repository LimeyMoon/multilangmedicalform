Vitiligo Area Scoring Index (VASI)

Instructions:

VASI is a semi-quantitative tool to measure the extent of vitiligo based on both body surface area (BSA) involved and the degree of depigmentation.

Steps:

  1. The body is divided into 6 regions: hands, upper limbs (excluding hands), trunk, lower limbs (excluding feet), feet, and head/neck.
  2. The area of vitiligo patches in each region is estimated as a percentage of that region’s surface area, using the patient’s palm (including fingers) as roughly 1% of total BSA.
  3. For each area, the degree of depigmentation is estimated as a percentage and categorized as:
    • 0%
    • 10%
    • 25%
    • 50%
    • 75%
    • 90%
    • 100%

VASI for each region = (% of region affected) × (% depigmentation)

Formula for each region:

Regional VASI = [Fractional area of region with vitiligo] × [Fractional depigmentation]

Interpretation:

  • Higher VASI = greater body involvement and depigmentation.
  • Use at baseline and during follow-up for objective comparison.
